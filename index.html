Interesting Facts about Camels


1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

2. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in.

3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

4. Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat

5. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Interesting Facts about Chameleons


1. There are more than 200 species of chameleons.

2. Chameleons have the fastest tongue speed.

3. Chameleons change their colors to express their moods.

4. Chameleons don't have a good sense of hearing.

5. Some female chameleon species can hatch their eggs inside their body.

Interesting Facts about Cicadas


1. Most 17 Year Cicadas have red eyes, but they can also have white, gray, blue , or multi-colored eyes.

2. There are about 3,000 known species of cicada worldwide, according to the Smithsonian Natural Museum of History.

3. For the most part, cicadas subsist on the fluids of living trees.

4. Cicadas spend most of their life under the ground.

5. Cicadas can reach 0.79 to 3 inches in length.

Interesting Facts about Pandas


1. Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago.

2. A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day!

3. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

4. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

5. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

Interesting Facts about Pigeons


1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

2. Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.

3. They understand space and time.

4. They can find their way back to the nest from 1300 miles away.

5. They saved thousands of humans during World War I and Wold War II

Interesting Facts about Bears


1. Brown bears in the wild are mainly active at dusk and at night.

2. The brown bear is the largest predator still living on the continent of Europe.

3. In the wild, brown bears can reach a maximum age of between 20 and 30 years. In captivity, they can get even older.

4. Bears have a particularly good nose: their sense of smell enables them to sniff food at a distance of several kilometres.

5. Despite their weight, the animals can cover short distances at speeds of up to 50 km/h.
